Fiction A Short Story Fiction

December 6, 2013

So I haven’t written any fiction in a while so I’m gonna give it a shot.

Plot: After months of dealing with his best friend falling into bad habits, Ted has become overly frustrated, even though he knows that Max has been working hard to repair their friendship.

Bounce. Bounce. Bounce. The sound of the rubber ball slamming the floor. The only noise I can hear right now. I clench my first in frustration. Another letter. He’s done this to me so many times. I look up as I hear the sound of a key in a lock. Just the TV. The thoughts race through my head, what am I going to say to him? Where could he be this time? Why letters? Why printed? How is he so damn functionally dysfunctional?

I slam the ball into the floor one more time before picking up the letter again. I scam over the words, watching them melt together into a slurry of darkness.

Ted,

I'm not coming back. Stop worrying about me. I'll be fine.

-Max

What makes him think this is the best way to go about things? I’ve spent so much time helping him and this is how he repays me? I stand up and walk to the kitchen. The orange juice feels good as it slides down my throat, chilling my insides as my head gets hotter. I rinse out the glass, remembering how angry Max always got when I left a little bit of dried juice on the bottom. I really shouldn’t be this angry. I really should just let it go. He always comes back.

I sit down on the couch and start channel flipping. Homework. I should be doing my homework. I could be doing homework. I’m not doing homework. The last thing I can focus on right now is a literary theory essay. He always does this to me, I wind up getting so engrossed in worrying about him that I blow off my other responsibilities.

I hear a knock at the door. It startles me a bit as I crash land back into reality. I jump around a bit as I make my way to the door. I swing it open. Jack stands in front of me.

“Hey. I forgot my keys. Sorry.”, he says. He tilts his head slightly to the right, squinting his eyes while furrowing his brow. I lean in and kiss him.

“No problem. I was just day dreaming anyway.”, I say, “I honestly needed a distraction.”

“Come on babe, you have to stop thinking about him.”, he lets out a breath as the words escape his mouth. He pauses for a few seconds as we stare emptily at each other. He hugs me. I don’t hug back. “Don’t be like this. You can’t let him ruin your life. Not again.”

I break out of his hug and walk back into the kitchen. The glass drying in the rack has an orange oval on the bottom. Fuck. Max would care about that, Jack doesn’t even know we have a kitchen. I take a deep breath. Time to buck up.

“How was work?”, I ask.

“Same old. Although there was this really weird guy who kept picking up books and putting them back in random places…” His words drift away as I refocus on the letter sitting on the coffee table. Where the hell is he.

“TED!”, Jack screams in my face.

“WHAT?!”, I scream back.

“Can you at least pretend to pay attention to me? I’m worried about you.”, he tilts his head again, “You’re not handling this well. Maybe you should get help?”

“I don’t need help. It made it worse the last time.”, I shake my head at him as the lie leaves my mouth. Talking to someone did help last time, but I want to wallow in my sadness. Maybe if I do, I’ll remember the pain when he tries to come back into my life.

“He’s dead.”, Jack says. I lock eyes with him, “He’s dead to all of us. Monica. You. Me. Sam. He’s left us. Move. On.”

I understand his words. I refuse to believe them. I refuse to believe anything this ridiculous. Max is coming back, he’ll be here in the morning and he’ll apologize to me and—

Jack slams his face into mine. Botched kiss as our foreheads collide. We both fall over the back of the couch, my arms holding him tightly. Our legs flail in the air as he kisses my neck. I sit there, still thinking about the possible places where Max could be. Jack bites me. Fuck it. I’m not letting Max ruin this too.

I wake up abruptly. Jack’s arm is wrapped around my waist as we lie in bed. What the fuck just happened? I turn around and face him. The blinking colon of the alarm clock breaks my gaze at his peaceful face. 2:00 AM. I take a short breath, kiss Jack on the forehead and walk out of our bedroom. I grab a glass out of the drying rack, still has an orange ring on the bottom. Fuck it. The light from the refrigerator blinds me as I feel around for the orange juice carton.

“It’s on the counter.”, I hear a voice say.

“Thanks. Why are you up so late?”, I respond as I pour a full glass and put the carton in the fridge.

“Same reason as usual. Sam’s crying his eyes out.”, the voice says again, “Jackie keeps punching things and calling me. Max needs to stop hurting people.”

I sit down next to Monica. Her mascara is smeared. Classic two weeks into a Max disappearance. At least she isn’t crying alone for the whole night this time. I put my arm around here.

“He’s going to come back.”, I say. I should probably stop lying to people, “He always does. Just don’t give him money this time…”

“That’s easy for you to say.”, Monica retorts. What the fuck is wrong with her.

“Totally. My best friend of 20 years suddenly starts disappearing, coming back home broke, and it’s easier for me than his one time girlfriend.”, I should be more careful with my words, but they’re already out there.

“Let’s me escalate this anymore. I don’t want Jack to come storming out here.”, she smiles, “Unless he’s still sleeping naked…”

“Lay off. He’s mine.”, I snap, “I’m surprised you didn’t bring something home tonight.”

“Can’t stop thinking about him.”, she cackles, “His name is Tom, and he passed out hours ago. He has a really nice—”

“And that’s my queue. I’ll listen to your sexcapades in the morning.”, I stand up and walk to the kitchen. I rinse out the glass as I speak, “I’m sure it’s wonderful. Don’t feel bad when I outpace you again.”

I put the glass in the drying rack. Still a damn orange oval. I walk into my bedroom.

“Goodnight Monica.”

“Goodnight Ted.”
