Environments Matter Workflow & Development

November 20, 2013

I used to hate reading books when I was younger. I’ve always been a slow reader, and I was much more interested in writing and creating something than reading what others had created. As I’ve grown up I’ve spent more time reading, but it’s been pretty painful because of the slow reader problem. While doing laundry today I realized something: environments matter.

Concentration

That’s the reason why I usually have trouble reading. Either there are too many distractions from the outside world or my mind won’t calm down enough for me to focus on the topic at hand. Sometimes it doesn’t even stop be from reading either. So I’ll read a few pages before I notice that I haven’t been processing what I’ve been reading and I’m actually just thinking about something completely different. Then I go back and reread what I just read. Frustration ensues.

While I was doing laundry this evening I decided to try and finish reading a book I’ve been working on for a couple weeks now. I’ve been taunted by the icon on the back that indicates it’s a 5 hour read. In about one and a half hours I managed to get to almost the end of the book, which amazed me. I felt myself enter a zone where I was in text.

I used to be able to do this when I had my awful hour and a half long commute to work. I would starting reading a book on the A train, and the time flew by as I became engrossed in the text. This commute was actually helpful, I managed to get through a large number of books over the course of a couple months because I had 3 hours a day to focus on reading. I don’t get the same results with my now 10 minute commute.

When the internet is in front of you, it’s hard to step away for 3 hours to focus on reading. There is a constant buzz in your pocket from email, friends wanting to hang out, and a newsfeed that needs tending too. I managed to let all that go while I chewed through this book.
